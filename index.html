About


Hi! I am Abhinav. I use this space to share thoughts on data, analytics, and the occasional half-baked experiment. It’s a growing collection of projects, ideas, and statistical explorations—some clean, some still debugging. Most of what I work on floats somewhere between Bayesian intuition and Frequentist discipline.

Some things that make me happy:learning new things, long conversations with close friends, watching Barcelona find magic on the weekends, statistics, movies, collecting stack of books and occasionally reading them, Kung Fu Panda for its unlikely wisdom, anime by Masashi Kishimoto and Hayao Miyazaki, and wandering into new tech stacks just for the joy of it.

I currently work at Pratham as an Associate in Research & Analytics, where I use data to solve real-world problems in education and development. Previously, I spent time experimenting with analytics, dashboards, and storytelling through numbers across various academic and field projects. I believe in the power of simple models, strong priors, and good visualizations.

I studied engineering as an undergraduate and have been busy re-learning (and occasionally unlearning) ever since. My work sits at the intersection of research, data, and AI tools—and occasionally, where football season overlaps with regression session.

The opinions shared here are statistically independent and solely my own.
